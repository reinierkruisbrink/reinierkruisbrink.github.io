# Immune: A Journey into the Mysterious System that Keeps You Alive

## Overview

"Immune" by Philip Dettmer is a beautiful introduction to the human immune system, its functions, and its impact on our health. Dettmer delves into the complex world of immunology, breaking down concepts in an accessible manner for readers of all backgrounds.

## Key Concepts
### Overview of Immune Function

- **Innate vs Adaptive**: Innate cells (macrophages, neutrophils, NK cells) provide rapid, non-specific defense; adaptive cells (B and T cells) build targeted, long-lasting responses.

### Key Mechanisms

- **Antibodies & Antigen Recognition**: B cells produce antibodies that neutralize pathogens; T cells recognize infected cells via MHC presentation.
- **Inflammation & Signalling**: Cytokines coordinate recruitment and activation of immune effectors; inflammation is protective but can be pathogenic when dysregulated.

### Memory and Dysregulation

- **Immunological Memory**: Vaccination and prior infection generate faster, stronger responses upon re-exposure.
- **Malfunctions**: Autoimmunity, allergies, and immunodeficiency arise when recognition or regulation breaks down.
