# Florapedia

## Overview

Florapedia is an illustrated compendium that celebrates the diversity and wonder of the plant world. The book features nearly 100 entries on a wide range of topics, from plant ecology and conservation to the role of plants in art, literature, and human culture.

## Key Concepts
### Plant Diversity and Form

- **Anatomy and Morphology**: Overview of flowers, roots, leaves, inflorescences and other structural variation.
  - **Adaptations**: Specialized forms (e.g., orchids mimicking pollinators, carnivorous traps).

### Plant–Animal Interactions

- **Co‑evolution**: Mutualisms such as figs and fig wasps demonstrate tight ecological partnerships.

### Plants in Culture and Art

- **Botanical Illustration & Lore**: The book celebrates historical and cultural representations of plants alongside natural history.

Gracie presents plant biology alongside cultural stories to showcase the wonder and utility of botanical life.
