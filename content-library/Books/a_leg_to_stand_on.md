# A Leg to Stand On

## Overview

In A Leg to Stand On, Oliver Sacks, the renowned neurologist, sheds his usual white coat and becomes the patient. The book chronicles his experience recovering from a severe leg injury sustained in a mountaineering accident.

## Key Concepts
### Clinical Perspective and Narrative

- **Physician as Patient**: Sacks combines clinical insight with first-person narrative to illuminate the lived experience of neurological injury.
  - **Case weaving**: Medical anecdotes contextualize his recovery within broader neurophysiological knowledge.

### Mind-Body Recovery

- **Neuroplasticity & Rehabilitation**: The book highlights how practice, sensory feedback, and therapeutic approaches (including music) support functional recovery.
  - **Psychological factors**: Motivation, attention, and belief shape rehabilitation outcomes.

### Embodiment and Selfhood

- **Altered Self-Perception**: Injury reveals how bodily integrity underpins identity and agency.
  - **Reflection**: Sacks uses his experience to probe how physical changes reshape subjective experience.

The memoir blends clinical explanation with human storytelling to show how biology, therapy, and personal resilience interact during recovery.
