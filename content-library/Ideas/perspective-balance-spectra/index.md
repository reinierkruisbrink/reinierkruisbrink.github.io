- Most things we treat as binary are actually spectra: health/illness, friend/enemy, order/chaos, natural/artificial.

- False dichotomies as rhetorical tools — and how to notice when a spectrum is being collapsed into an either/or.
- Perspective as a physical metaphor: parallax — two observers at different positions see different things, both correctly.
- The Overton window: what counts as "balance" or "the middle" is itself a political position, not a neutral one.
- When balance is not the goal: sometimes the right answer is at an extreme (zero tolerance for certain things, full commitment to others).
- Spectra and moral reasoning: is moral relativism just perspective-taking taken too far? Where does it break down?
