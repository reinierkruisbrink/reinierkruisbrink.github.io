Seed ideas:
- The core tension: markets are good at pricing what can be measured and traded now; sustainability requires pricing what is diffuse, future, and shared.
- Externalities as the structural problem — not a market failure but a feature of how markets are defined (what counts as a cost?).
- True Cost Accounting (TCA): the attempt to internalise externalities — what would prices look like if soil depletion, carbon, water use were included?
- Discount rates: a 5% discount rate means the world in 50 years is worth almost nothing today. This is not neutral — it's a moral assumption.
- The commons: Ostrom's work shows commons can be governed without either privatisation or state control — under specific conditions.
- Degrowth vs green growth: can decoupling (more GDP, less resource use) actually happen fast enough, or does growth structurally require throughput?
- Can markets be redesigned, or do they need to be bounded by non-market institutions to serve sustainability goals?
