Seed ideas:
- Clarkson's line: "Speed doesn't kill you — suddenly becoming stationary does." The danger is in the rate of change, not the state.
- Punctuated equilibrium vs gradualism: most change in nature and history is not smooth — long plateaus punctuated by rapid shifts.
- Phase transitions: water doesn't slowly become ice, it snaps. Many social and ecological systems behave the same way (tipping points).
- Invasive species as a case study: the lag between introduction and ecological collapse — invisible change followed by abrupt visible change.
- Adaptation takes time — fast change strips systems of the ability to respond, whether ecosystems, organisations, or people.
- Why we're bad at perceiving slow change (boiling frog) and over-react to fast change (news cycles, panic).
- Tempo as a design variable: in music, cooking, urban planning — pacing shapes experience and outcome.
